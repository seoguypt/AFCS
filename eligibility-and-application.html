Eligibility and Application for the Armed Forces Compensation Scheme

Understanding AFCS Eligibility

The Armed Forces Compensation Scheme (AFCS) is designed to provide compensation to service personnel and veterans who have suffered injuries or illnesses as a result of their service in the UK Armed Forces. Understanding the eligibility criteria is crucial for a successful application.

Service Requirements

To be eligible for the AFCS, you must have been a serving member of the UK Armed Forces at the time of the incident that caused your injury or illness. This includes:

  • Regular forces
  • Reserve forces
  • Royal Fleet Auxiliary

It's important to note that the scheme covers injuries or illnesses that occurred on or after 6 April 2005. For incidents before this date, you may be eligible under the War Pension Scheme.

Medical Fitness

The AFCS considers the impact of your injury or illness on your ability to perform your duties and your overall quality of life. Key points to consider include:

  • The injury or illness must be caused by service
  • Pre-existing conditions may be considered if they were made worse by service
  • Mental health conditions are also covered under the scheme

A thorough medical assessment is a crucial part of the application process. Our team can help you gather the necessary medical evidence to support your claim.

Application Deadlines

Understanding the time limits for AFCS applications is crucial:

  • You have 7 years from the date of injury or illness to make a claim
  • For illnesses, the 7-year period starts from the date of diagnosis or the start of medical treatment
  • In cases of late onset illness, you can claim at any time after leaving service

It's important to act promptly, but don't worry if you're approaching the deadline. Our experts can help you expedite your application process.

The Application Process

Applying for the AFCS involves several steps:

  1. Gathering necessary documentation (medical records, service history)
  2. Completing the application form
  3. Submitting the application to Veterans UK
  4. Attending medical assessments if required
  5. Awaiting the decision

Our team can guide you through each of these steps, ensuring your application is as strong as possible.

Appeal Process

If your initial application is unsuccessful, you have the right to appeal. The appeal process includes:

  • Requesting a review of the decision within 12 months
  • Providing additional evidence to support your claim
  • Appealing to an independent tribunal if necessary

Our experts have extensive experience in handling AFCS appeals and can provide valuable support throughout this process.
