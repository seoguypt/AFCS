Types of Compensation Under the Armed Forces Compensation Scheme

Overview of AFCS Compensation

The Armed Forces Compensation Scheme (AFCS) offers various types of compensation to support service personnel and veterans who have suffered injuries or illnesses due to their service. Understanding these different types of compensation is crucial for ensuring you receive the full support you're entitled to.

Disability Pensions

Disability pensions are a key component of the AFCS, providing ongoing financial support to those with service-related disabilities. Key points include:

  • Guaranteed Income Payments (GIP) for more severe injuries
  • Lump sum payments for less severe injuries
  • Payments based on the severity of the injury and its impact on earning capacity

Our team can help you understand which type of disability pension you may be eligible for and assist in maximising your compensation.

Rehabilitation Allowances

Rehabilitation allowances are designed to support your recovery and reintegration. These can include:

  • Funding for physical rehabilitation programmes
  • Support for vocational training and career transition
  • Allowances for necessary home adaptations

We can guide you through the process of accessing these allowances and ensuring you receive the full range of rehabilitation support available.

Bereavement Benefits

The AFCS also provides support for families of service personnel who have died as a result of service. Bereavement benefits can include:

  • Survivor's Guaranteed Income Payment (SGIP)
  • Child's Payment for eligible dependent children
  • Bereavement Grant to help with immediate costs

Our compassionate team can provide guidance and support to families navigating these difficult circumstances.

Medical Expenses

The AFCS can cover various medical expenses related to your service-induced injury or illness:

  • Costs of ongoing medical treatment
  • Expenses for specialist equipment
  • Travel costs for medical appointments

We can help you identify and claim all eligible medical expenses to ensure you receive comprehensive care without financial burden.

Career Transition Services

For those whose injuries necessitate a career change, the AFCS offers support for career transition:

  • Access to vocational assessments
  • Funding for retraining programmes
  • Support for job search and placement

Our experts can guide you through accessing these services and planning your career transition effectively.

Additional Compensation Elements

The AFCS may also provide compensation for:

  • Loss of earnings capacity
  • Pain and suffering
  • Impact on quality of life

These elements are considered in the overall compensation package, and our team can help ensure all aspects of your case are properly evaluated.
